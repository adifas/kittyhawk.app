Space Quest

A progressive journey to Low Earth Orbit through certified rocketry development and launches.

Progressive Certification

Start with Level 1 certification (H and I impulse motors) and progress through advanced stages for suborbital and orbital launches.

Multi-Stage Journey

Structured program starting with F-class motors, progressing through suborbital stages, and culminating in Low Earth Orbit missions.

Safety First

Comprehensive safety protocols and certification requirements at each stage of development.

Certification Levels

Level 1

Certification for H and I impulse class motors (solid and hybrid). Some F and G motors may require this level.

Level 2

Advanced certification for J, K, and L impulse class motors, enabling larger payloads and higher altitudes.

Level 3

Expert certification for M, N, and O impulse class motors, required for suborbital missions.

Level 4

Special certification for orbital launches requiring government permits and advanced propulsion systems.

Program Timeline

Initial Phase

  • Months 1-6:

    Level 1 certification and F/G class motor launches

  • Months 7-12:

    Level 2 certification and H-K class motor launches

Advanced Phase

  • Months 13-15:

    Level 3 certification and L-O class motor launches (Suborbital)

  • Months 16-18:

    Level 4 certification and Low Earth Orbit launch preparation

Special Permissions & Requirements

Government Permissions

  • Level 4 Requirements:

    Special permits from aviation and space authorities

  • Launch Site Approval:

    Certified launch facility with orbital capabilities

  • Environmental Impact:

    Environmental assessment and mitigation plans

Technical Requirements

  • Advanced Propulsion:

    Multi-stage rocket systems with orbital capabilities

  • Safety Systems:

    Advanced flight termination and recovery systems

  • Mission Control:

    Certified mission control facilities and procedures
